Section 1 - 1:30
1
Part 1

The Goal

One or two sentences describing what you want to build and who it's for. Be specific. "Build me an app" is too vague.

Example

"I want to build a learning portal that helps non-programmers learn AI coding and ship their ideas using Claude."

Section 1 - 2:00
2
Part 2

The Constraints

Boundaries and requirements. What must be included? What's the target audience? Any limitations? Constraints help Claude make better decisions.

Example

  • Target: absolute beginners, no coding experience
  • Must include: landing page, blog, video courses
  • Display course series on the home page
  • Content managed through simple files
Section 1 - 2:30
3
Part 3

Tech Preference

It's okay to say "no preference" or "something simple." Claude will suggest technologies if you don't specify.

Example

  • Next.js with TypeScript
  • Tailwind CSS for styling
  • Deployed on Vercel
Or simply: "No preference - something simple I can run in a browser"
Section 1 - 3:00
4
Part 4

Success Criteria

How will you know the project worked? This is your checklist for "done." It prevents scope creep and keeps both you and Claude focused.

Example

  • Users can browse and watch course videos
  • Users can navigate between lessons easily
  • Site loads fast and looks professional
  • Easy to add new courses and lessons
Section 2 - 3:30

The Secret Weapon

One line that dramatically improves Claude's output

"Before generating anything, please ask me clarifying questions."

Instead of guessing, Claude will ask you smart questions first. Your answers become additional context for much better results.

Section 3 - 4:30

The Complete 02Ship Prompt

Goal:
I want to build a learning portal that helps non-programmers learn AI coding and ship their ideas using Claude.

Constraints:
- Target audience: absolute beginners
- Must include: landing page, blog, video courses
- Display course series on the home page
- Content managed through simple files

Tech Preference:
Next.js + TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, Vercel

Success Criteria:
- Users can browse and watch course videos
- Easy navigation between lessons
- Fast and professional-looking

Before generating anything, please ask me clarifying questions.

Section 4 - 7:00

Reviewing Claude's Response

You're reviewing the plan, not the engineering

1
Does the project structure look logical?
2
Does the feature list match what you asked for?
3
Do the technology choices align with your preference?
4
Is there a clear step-by-step plan?
5
Is anything missing from your requirements?

Section 5 - 10:00

The Power of Iteration

No first response is perfect. Clear, specific feedback makes the next version better.

"This looks great, but I'd like the landing page to have a stronger hero section with a clear call-to-action."

Before

  • Basic landing page
  • Simple course listing
  • No community links

After One Round

  • Compelling hero section + CTA
  • Lessons with learning objectives
  • Discord + GitHub in nav and footer

Closing - 13:30

Key Takeaways

1

Good prompts have four parts: goal, constraints, tech, success criteria

2

"Ask clarifying questions" is the secret weapon

3

Review the plan, not the code. Blueprint, not engineering.

4

Iteration is specific feedback, not "make it better"
