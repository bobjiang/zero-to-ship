Section 3 - 4:30
Phases 1 & 2

Gather & Summarize

Gather: Find 3-5 articles, reports, blog posts, or Reddit threads. Copy key paragraphs into Claude -- it cannot browse the web.

Summarize: Ask Claude to summarize each source with a specific focus so the summaries are useful, not generic.

Example Prompt
"Summarize each of these sources in 3-5 bullet points, focusing on user adoption trends."
Key: Tell Claude WHAT to focus on. That focus makes summaries useful.
Section 3 - 5:15
Phases 3 & 4

Synthesize & Insights

Synthesize: This is where the magic happens. Claude finds common themes, disagreements, and gaps across your sources.

Insights: Ask for actionable takeaways -- not just understanding, but "what should I do about it?"

Synthesis Prompt
"Synthesize these summaries. What are the common themes? Where do sources disagree? What's missing?"
Insights Prompt
"What are the top 3 insights for someone building a learning platform for beginners? What should I do next?"
Section 4 - 6:00

Research Prompts for Different Situations

📈 Market Research

"Based on these articles about the online education market, what's the market size, who are the main players, and what trends should I pay attention to?"

⚖️ Competitor Research

"Create a comparison table showing pricing, features, target audience, strengths, and weaknesses. Then tell me where the gap is."

👥 User Research

"What are their top frustrations? What do they wish existed? What language do they use to describe their problems?"

🛠️ Technical Research

"What are the pros and cons for a beginner-focused learning platform? What alternatives should I consider?"

Section 5 - 10:15

Important Caveats

✅

Claude Excels At

Pattern recognition, synthesis, theme identification, connecting ideas across sources

⚠️

Always Verify

Specific numbers, dates, statistics, and factual claims. Claude's knowledge has a cutoff date.

📚

You Provide

The source material. Claude cannot browse the internet -- you paste content in for analysis.

💡

Best Practice

Use Claude for patterns and insights, then spot-check the critical facts yourself.
