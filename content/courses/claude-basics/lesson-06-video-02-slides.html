Two Types of Data You'll Encounter

🔢

Quantitative

= Numbers

  • Page views & sign-up counts
  • Conversion rates
  • Survey ratings (1-5 scale)
  • Revenue & time on page
💬

Qualitative

= Words & Opinions

  • User feedback & reviews
  • Interview transcripts
  • Support tickets
  • Discord & forum comments

Claude handles both. You just need different questions.

Section 2 - 2:30

How to Get Your Data Into Claude

RECOMMENDED
📄

CSV Format

Export from Google Sheets or Excel. Copy and paste the text into Claude. The gold standard for numerical data.

📋

Tables

Copy-paste tables directly from spreadsheets or web pages. Claude handles them well.

📝

Plain Text

Describe your data in words: "I have 50 responses. Average score is 4.2." Works for smaller datasets.

Section 3 - 4:00

Asking the Right Analysis Questions

❌

The Wrong Way

"Analyze this."

Like handing someone a book and saying "read this" -- they won't know what you're looking for.

✅

The Right Way

Ask specific questions

Patterns, trends, outliers, and recommendations -- each unlocks different insights.

Section 3 - 4:30

Four Types of Analysis Questions

🔎 Pattern Questions

"What patterns do you see?"

"What's the most common response?"

"How do numbers change over time?"

📈 Trend Questions

"Are numbers going up or down?"

"Is satisfaction improving?"

"Which feature gets more mentions?"

🔴 Outlier Questions

"What's unusual in this data?"

"Which responses stand out?"

"What doesn't fit the pattern?"

🎯 Recommendation Questions

"What should I prioritize?"

"If I can fix one thing, what?"

"What should I do next?"

Section 4 - 8:30

Pro Tip: Ask Claude to Quote Evidence

Asking Claude to quote specific responses prevents unsupported generalizations. You see the "why" behind patterns, not just conclusions.

Key Technique

Always ask: "Quote specific responses that illustrate each point."

Section 5 - 10:00

When Claude Is Enough vs. When You Need an Expert

Claude Is Enough For...

  • Identifying themes in user feedback
  • Spotting general trends in metrics
  • Quick survey analysis
  • Early-stage product decisions
  • Generating hypotheses to test

Covers 90% of beginner needs

Get Expert Help When...

  • High-stakes financial decisions
  • Statistically rigorous A/B tests
  • Regulated data (medical, legal)
  • Patterns are confusing/contradictory
  • Compliance requirements apply

The other 10% -- important but rare
