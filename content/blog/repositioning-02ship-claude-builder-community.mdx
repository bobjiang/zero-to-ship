# Repositioning 02Ship as a Claude Builder Community

When we started 02Ship, the focus was courses — teaching non-programmers how to build with AI coding tools. That's still core to what we do. But something interesting happened: people didn't just want to learn. They wanted to build together, show what they made, and get feedback from others on the same journey.

So we're leaning into that.

## From Course Platform to Community

02Ship is evolving into a **Claude builder community**. The courses aren't going away — they're becoming one part of a bigger picture that includes:

- **Project Hub** — a showcase where community members display what they've built
- **Cohorts** — structured ship weeks where a group builds and ships together
- **Meetups** — in-person and online events for learning and sharing
- **Daily News** — curated AI news to keep everyone informed

## The Project Hub Model

The Project Hub (our `/ships` page) is the centerpiece. Instead of just consuming content, community members become creators. Build something, submit it, and it gets featured on the site.

This creates a virtuous cycle: new members see what others have built, get inspired, join a cohort, build their own project, and submit it to the hub. The community feeds itself.

## Cohorts Over Solo Learning

We've found that people are more likely to finish a project when they're building alongside others. Ship weeks give structure — a start date, an end date, and a group of people all pushing toward the same deadline.

It's not a bootcamp. There's no curriculum to follow. You pick your own project, use whatever AI tools you want, and ship by the end of the week. The community provides accountability and support.

## What This Means for You

If you've been thinking about building something with AI tools but haven't started — this is your invitation. Join a [ship week](/ship-weeks), build something small, and [submit it](/ships) when you're done. You don't need to be a programmer. That's the whole point.
